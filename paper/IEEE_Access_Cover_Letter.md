Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript entitled **"ZSH: A Zeta-Weighted Hybrid Semantic Clustering Framework for Blockchain Transaction Profiling and Anomaly-Aware Forensic Analysis"** for publication in *IEEE Access*.

This manuscript presents a large-scale blockchain analytics framework for transaction profiling and anomaly-aware forensic interpretation. Using 11,303,526 Bitcoin transactions described by 27 behavioral and structural features, the proposed framework produces 30 semantically interpretable transaction profiles and a separate anomaly layer. The paper is positioned around a deliberately careful and evidence-based claim. In the corrected same-space benchmark, `KMeans++ Elkan` remains stronger on intrinsic Euclidean clustering geometry; however, the proposed `ZSH` framework provides stronger semantic profiling utility, including higher semantic purity, lower entropy, more high-purity profiles, and better minority-profile recoverability under shallow expert-rule explanation. We believe this task-aligned framing is appropriate for the practical goals of blockchain forensics, compliance analytics, and behavioral intelligence.

The contribution of the article is fourfold. First, it introduces a finite-normalized Riemann zeta weighting mechanism for blockchain transaction features. Second, it integrates semantic seed allocation, hybrid clustering, and profile-preserving anomaly detection into a single profiling pipeline. Third, it evaluates the method using a corrected same-space protocol with bootstrap confidence intervals, permutation testing, baseline comparison, and ablation analysis. Fourth, it provides a reproducibility-oriented artifact package and a public-code-ready repository structure to support experimentation transparency.

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and its submission to *IEEE Access*.
